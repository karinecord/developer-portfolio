Discover 3 Must-Visit Destinations in Busan

Busan’s attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples scattered across the city’s mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers plenty to do year-round—families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to panoramic viewpoints. The Buddhist temples along Busan’s coast and mountains feature stunning architecture that will delight photographers.

  1. Templo Haedong Yonggungsa

    Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeastern tip of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few seaside temples in Korea—you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.

    2. Best for:

    • History

  2. Beomeosa Temple

    Beomeosa Temple is one of the largest shrines in South Korea. It is located high on the eastern edge of Geumjeongsan Mountain, away from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple’s Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.

    3. Best for:

    • History

  3. Yongdusan Park

    Yongdusan Park, located in the heart of Busan, is home to some of the city’s most important landmarks. Visitors can enjoy spectacular views from the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park also features two museums—explore traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and more than 80 model Korean ships at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.

    4. Best for:

    • Casais
    • Famílias
    • Orçamento

The best things to do in Busan reflect the city’s reputation as a major maritime hub in Asia. Often seen as the essence of South Korea, you’ll experience a unique atmosphere of ethnic and cultural diversity, as the city welcomes a cosmopolitan crowd year-round.
